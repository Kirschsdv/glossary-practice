Study Guide

How to use this tool for maximum exam impact — based on CAPS and past papers

How your history exam is structured
The 2023 national exemplar is 75 marks over 120 minutes (plus 15 min reading time). Every exam has the same three sections.
Section 1 — Multiple choice
~30 marks · 1 mark each
Source-based multiple choice. Tests recall and reading comprehension. Must-know terms dominate this section.
Section 2 — Source analysis
~25 marks · 2–4 marks per question
Analyse 3–4 sources. Common tasks: explain, describe, identify bias, compare. Exam instruction words are critical here.
Section 3 — Extended writing
~20 marks · 8–10 marks per essay
Structured paragraph or short essay. Marked on facts (6 points), intro sentence, conclusion sentence. High-priority terms are the facts examiners look for.
What cognitive level are questions testing?
CAPS requires every exam to be weighted across three cognitive levels.

Top tips for Afrikaans history exams
  • Read the instruction word first. In Afrikaans exams the instruction word tells you exactly what kind of answer is expected. Get it wrong and you lose marks even if you know the content.
  • "Gebruik Bron A en u eie kennis" means you MUST use both. If you only quote the source, you get half marks. Always quote something specific from the source AND add a fact not in the source.
  • For "verduidelik" questions, use the three-part structure: (1) State WHAT happened. (2) Explain WHY. (3) State WHAT the result was.
  • For the essay question, the marking memo rewards structure. You get marks for an opening sentence, 6 factual points, and a concluding sentence.
  • Bias questions always follow the same pattern. State that the source IS biased. Quote the specific word or phrase. Explain WHO wrote it and WHY that makes it one-sided.
  • Use the 15-minute reading time. Underline terms you recognise, circle instruction words, and mark which questions you will answer first.
  • The Must-know filter in the Quiz is your exam simulator. If you can answer every Must-know term correctly in Fill in blank mode, you are ready for the exam.

How to Study EMS

A step-by-step study plan proven to get every term cold — in both languages

1
Read first, quiz second
Do this before your first quiz session

Open the Glossary tab and work through one topic at a time. For each card read the English and Afrikaans terms out loud, then click to open and read both definitions.

  • Pay extra attention to ★ Must-know cards — these appear in almost every exam.
  • For Exam Language, actively memorise what each instruction word requires. Misreading verduidelik as noem costs you marks.
💡 TipStart with the topic your next test covers. Use the filter chips to focus on just that section.
2
Build recognition with Flashcards
Low pressure, high exposure

Switch to the Quiz tab, select Flashcards, and run through all terms for your topic. The goal is exposure, not scoring.

  • Be honest when rating yourself. If you hesitated, tap Didn't know.
  • Set direction to English → Afrikaans first, then repeat with Afrikaans → English.
💡 TipA term is only mastered after 3 correct answers across sessions. That's your real progress number.
3
Test with Multiple Choice
Simulates real exam conditions

Multiple choice is closest to how Section A of your paper is structured. Use Mixed direction since the exam won't tell you which way the question will come from.

💡 TipAfter a session, review the terms marked ✗. Those are your priority for the next session.
4
Cement it with Fill in the Blank
Use this when you feel confident

Fill in the blank forces active recall — you produce the answer from memory. Set direction to Definition → Term to mirror "Name the term…" exam questions.

💡 TipIf you can pass Fill in the Blank at 80%+ with Mixed directions, you are exam-ready for that topic.
5
The day before your exam
Final review strategy

Do not try to learn new things. Your goal is to activate what you already know.

  • Read through the Exam Language section in full. Say each instruction word aloud.
  • Run one Flashcard session on All topics with Mixed direction — 20 cards.
  • Run one Multiple Choice session with Focus on missed terms enabled.
  • Stop studying at least 1 hour before bed.
🔥 Streak goalStudy for at least 10–15 minutes every day. Spaced repetition is the most effective study method.
